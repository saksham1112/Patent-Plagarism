Common Questions

Everything you need to know about using PatentCheck to protect your intellectual property.

What types of patents can PatentCheck analyze?

PatentCheck can analyze utility patents, design patents, plant patents, and specialized categories including software/algorithm patents, mechanical, chemical, electrical/electronic, and biotechnology patents. Our system is trained to understand technical language across all major patent categories.

How accurate is your plagiarism detection?

Our AI-powered system achieves 98.7% accuracy in detecting direct text matches and 94.2% accuracy in identifying conceptual similarities based on independent testing. The system continuously improves through machine learning as it processes more patents. For optimal results, we recommend providing complete patent documents including all claims and technical descriptions.

Which patent databases do you search against?

We search against comprehensive databases including USPTO (United States), EPO (Europe), WIPO (International), JPO (Japan), KIPO (Korea), and CNIPA (China). Our system currently indexes over 120 million patents worldwide, with updates occurring weekly as new patents are published.

How long does an analysis typically take?

Analysis time depends on patent complexity and length. Most standard patents (10-20 pages) complete analysis in 2-5 minutes. Very complex patents (50+ pages) may take up to 15 minutes. You'll receive an email notification when your report is ready if you choose to step away during processing.

What file formats do you accept for upload?

We accept PDF (preferred for maintaining formatting), DOCX (Microsoft Word), and plain text (TXT) files. For design patents, we also accept image files (JPEG, PNG) with embedded text descriptions. The maximum file size is 25MB.

How do you handle my patent data and privacy?

All uploaded patents are encrypted in transit and at rest. Unpublished patents are never stored in our searchable database unless you explicitly grant permission. We offer NDAs for sensitive materials and comply with all major data protection regulations including GDPR and CCPA.

What does the similarity score mean?

Our similarity score (0-100%) represents the percentage of your patent's claims that show significant overlap with existing patents. Scores below 30% indicate minimal overlap, 30-70% suggest some conceptual similarity, and above 70% indicates potential infringement risk. Each match is annotated with specific problematic sections.

Can I use PatentCheck before filing a provisional patent?

Absolutely! In fact, we recommend using our service before any patent filing to identify potential conflicts early. Many users run multiple checks during the drafting process to iteratively improve their applications and strengthen their claims.

Do you offer enterprise solutions for law firms?

Yes, we offer customized enterprise solutions for law firms and corporate legal departments. These include API access, bulk processing capabilities, dedicated support, and custom integration with your existing systems. Contact our sales team to discuss enterprise options.

How does PatentCheck compare to traditional patent searches?

Traditional patent searches rely on keyword matching and manual review, which can miss conceptual similarities and are time-consuming. PatentCheck uses AI to understand the technical meaning behind claims, detects conceptual overlaps, and processes documents 10x faster than manual review while maintaining higher accuracy.
